Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 2026

ElderCare Guardian ("we", "our", "the app") respects your privacy. This policy explains how health data is collected, stored, and protected when you use our wearable monitoring application, in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) of India.

1. Data We Collect

  • Heart rate & SpO2: Captured from the MAX30102 sensor on the ESP32-C3 wearable.
  • Motion data: 6-axis accelerometer + gyroscope readings from the MPU-6050, used exclusively for fall detection.
  • Skin temperature: Measured when the sensor is available.
  • SOS events: Button press timestamps and alert acknowledgements.
  • Device identifiers: BLE MAC address (used only for connection; never transmitted off-device).

2. How Data Is Stored

  • Encrypted at rest: All health data is stored in a SQLCipher database (AES-256-GCM) on the device. The encryption key is held in Android Keystore (hardware-backed on supported devices).
  • No cloud storage: Health data never leaves the device. No cloud backup, sync, or remote server receives your vitals.
  • Automatic deletion: Alert events are purged after 7 days. Health snapshots are retained for 30 days.

3. How Data Is Used

  • Real-time fall risk assessment and caregiver alerts (on-device ML).
  • Vitals trend display on the dashboard (heart rate, SpO2, respiratory rate).
  • Optional SMS and push notification (FCM) alerts to the caregiver's phone — these contain only the alert level and reason, never raw vitals.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your health data with third parties. The only data transmitted off-device is:

  • SMS alerts (if enabled): Sent via the device's cellular radio to the caregiver's phone number.
  • FCM push notifications (if configured): Routed through Firebase Cloud Messaging to the caregiver's device. Google receives only the device registration token and alert metadata; no health readings are included.

5. Your Rights Under DPDPA

  • Right to access: Export all your data as a JSON file from the Settings screen.
  • Right to erasure: Delete all stored data from Settings. This clears the database, resets preferences, and revokes consent.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Revoke data collection consent at any time. The app will stop collecting health data.
  • Right to grievance redressal: Contact us at the address below.

6. Data Retention

  • Alert events: 7 days (auto-deleted).
  • Health snapshots: 30 days (auto-deleted).
  • Patient profiles: Kept until manually deleted.

7. Security Measures

  • Database: AES-256-GCM via SQLCipher, key stored in Android Keystore.
  • BLE communication: Bonded connections with MITM protection and numeric comparison.
  • App binary: ProGuard-obfuscated in release builds.

8. Changes to This Policy

We will update this policy as the app evolves. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
